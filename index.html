Danielle A. Cuilli

RESEARCH TECHNOLOGIST

Danielle Cuilli is an ambitious, conscientious researcher with strengths in problem-solving, attention-to-detail, and analytical writing. Her primary professional objective is the identification and development of cause-based treatments and/or cures for genetic, generalized epilepsies (GGEs). This article will provide a summary of the skills and relevant experience that DC has gained in pursuit of this goal.

Introduction

Danielle Cuilli’s interest in epilepsy research began when she was diagnosed with juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (JME) at 14 years-old (1999). At the time of her diagnosis, doctors assured her that her seizures could be easily controlled with existing anticonvulsant medications (ACs). However, after several pharmacological interventions failed to control her symptoms, her condition was eventually labeled as “intractable”. Although it was no longer expected that her epilepsy would respond to medication, doctors continued to make changes to her treatment in the years that followed.

As a result of her diagnosis, Danielle developed a passionate interest in the physiological manifestations of seizure disorders, and sought to understand both the potential causes of her epilepsy and the pharmacological mechanisms that are traditionally used for its treatment. This interest drove her to pursue a career in neuroscience research. She has carefully selected educational opportunities and research positions in effort to enhance her knowledge of the laboratory techniques, experimental designs, and statistical methods that are most relevant to the study of epilepsy.

In 2019, nearly 20 years after her initial diagnosis, Danielle’s doctors initiated a treatment regimen that resulted in complete seizure control. This development led to a paradigm-shift in Danielle's approach to epilepsy research, and will be further evaluated in the discussion.

Discussion

Danielle’s interest in personalized medicine is a relatively new component of her long-term goals—namely, it is the result of the 2019 discovery of a treatment regimen that effectively controlled her seizures. As an epilepsy patient, Danielle was exposed to the considerable level of uncertainty that physicians face when treating their patients.

Without a clear, data-driven path for selecting treatments for individual patients, a vast array of therapeutic options becomes a disadvantage for both the healthcare provider and for the patient. - calculate and discuss the probability of choosing “the one working drug” by chance>, - calculate and discuss the probability of choosing “the one working drug” within 20 year->…these will obviously be very small p values, ergo, we need to NOT do this by chance. We need to identify patterns—i.e., Danielle wants to get into data science.
